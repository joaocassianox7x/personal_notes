Functions and Domains
=====================

Definition
----------
A function :math:`f : A \to B` assigns each :math:`x \in A` exactly one :math:`f(x) \in B`.

- Domain: all :math:`x` where :math:`f(x)` is defined.
- Codomain :math:`B` vs. range (actual outputs).

Example
-------
:math:`f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x-2}` has domain :math:`\mathbb{R} \setminus \{2\}` and range :math:`\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}`.

Notes
-----
- Composition: :math:`(g \circ f)(x) = g(f(x))` is defined where the output of :math:`f` lies in the domain of :math:`g`.
- Inverses: :math:`f^{-1}` exists only if :math:`f` is bijective on its domain.
