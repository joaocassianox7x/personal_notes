Functions and Domains

Definition

A function \(f : A \to B\) assigns each \(x \in A\) exactly one \(f(x) \in B\).

  • Domain: all \(x\) where \(f(x)\) is defined.

  • Codomain \(B\) vs. range (actual outputs).

Example

\(f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x-2}\) has domain \(\mathbb{R} \setminus \{2\}\) and range \(\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}\).

Notes

  • Composition: \((g \circ f)(x) = g(f(x))\) is defined where the output of \(f\) lies in the domain of \(g\).

  • Inverses: \(f^{-1}\) exists only if \(f\) is bijective on its domain.
