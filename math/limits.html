Limits

Intuitive view

\(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L\) means \(f(x)\) can be made arbitrarily close to \(L\) by taking \(x\) sufficiently close to \(a\) (not necessarily equal).

Epsilon–delta definition

For all \(\varepsilon > 0\), there exists \(\delta > 0\) such that if \(0 < |x - a| < \delta\), then \(|f(x) - L| < \varepsilon\).

Basic facts

  • Linearity: \(\lim (a f + b g) = a \lim f + b \lim g\) (when limits exist).

  • Product: \(\lim (f g) = (\lim f)(\lim g)\).

  • Quotient: \(\lim (f / g) = (\lim f) / (\lim g)\) if \(\lim g \neq 0\).

  • Squeeze: if \(g(x) \le f(x) \le h(x)\) and \(\lim g = \lim h = L\), then \(\lim f = L\).

One-sided limits

  • Right-hand: \(\lim_{x \to a^+} f(x)\).

  • Left-hand: \(\lim_{x \to a^-} f(x)\).

The (two-sided) limit exists iff both one-sided limits exist and are equal.

Common limit tricks

  • Factor and cancel to resolve indeterminate forms.

  • Multiply by a conjugate for expressions with roots.

  • Use special limits: \(\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin x}{x} = 1\), \(\lim_{x \to 0} (1 + x)^{1/x} = e\).
