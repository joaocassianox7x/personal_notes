Quantum Harmonic Oscillator

Setup and potential

1D harmonic potential: \(V(x) = \tfrac{1}{2} m \omega^2 x^2\), a symmetric parabola centered at \(x=0\). Stationary Schr"odinger equation

\[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{d^2 \psi}{dx^2} + \tfrac{1}{2} m \omega^2 x^2 \psi = E \psi.\]

Step-by-step wavefunction solution

  1. Non-dimensionalize with \(\xi = \sqrt{\tfrac{m\omega}{\hbar}}\,x\) to simplify constants.

  2. Rewrite the equation as \(\psi''(\xi) - \xi^2 \psi(\xi) + 2\epsilon\,\psi(\xi) = 0\) with \(\epsilon = E/(\hbar\omega)\).

  3. Impose normalizability by factoring \(\psi(\xi) = h(\xi) e^{-\xi^2/2}\); then \(h(\xi)\) satisfies Hermite’s equation.

  4. Polynomial solutions require \(2\epsilon = 2n + 1\), giving energies \(E_n = \hbar \omega (n + 1/2)\).

  5. Normalized eigenfunctions become

\[\psi_n(x) = \left(\frac{m\omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1/4} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2^n n!}}\, H_n(\xi)\, e^{-\xi^2/2}, \quad \xi = \sqrt{\tfrac{m\omega}{\hbar}} x.\]

Step-by-step ladder-operator (Dirac) solution

  1. Define

\[a = \sqrt{\tfrac{m\omega}{2\hbar}}\,x + \frac{i}{\sqrt{2m\hbar\omega}}\,p, \qquad a^\dagger = \sqrt{\tfrac{m\omega}{2\hbar}}\,x - \frac{i}{\sqrt{2m\hbar\omega}}\,p.\]

  1. Verify commutator: \([a,a^\dagger]=1\) from \([x,p]=i\hbar\).

  2. Rewrite Hamiltonian: \(H = \hbar \omega (a^\dagger a + 1/2)\).

  3. Define ground state by \(a|0\rangle = 0\); its energy is \(E_0 = \tfrac{1}{2}\hbar\omega\).

  4. Excited states arise algebraically: \(|n\rangle = (a^\dagger)^n / \sqrt{n!} \, |0\rangle\) with \(H|n\rangle = \hbar\omega(n+1/2)|n\rangle\).

Remarks

  • Wavefunctions are localized by the Gaussian factor; increasing \(n\) adds nodes via \(H_n\).

  • Equally spaced levels imply constant transition energy \(\hbar\omega\).

  • In 3D, degeneracy grows with total quantum number; creation/annihilation operators generalize componentwise.
